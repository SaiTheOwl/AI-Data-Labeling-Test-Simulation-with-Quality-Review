# AI Data Labeling Test Simulation with Quality Review

## 📌 Overview

This project simulates a real-world AI data labeling evaluation similar to those used in hiring processes for data annotation roles. It focuses on sentiment classification, topic categorization, and confidence scoring across a variety of standard, ambiguous, and edge-case scenarios.

The goal of this project is to demonstrate the ability to apply annotation guidelines consistently, handle ambiguous inputs, and maintain high-quality labeled data—key skills required in AI training workflows.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Apply structured annotation guidelines to classify text data
* Maintain consistency across similar and edge-case inputs
* Demonstrate reasoning for sentiment and topic classification
* Perform quality review to validate labeling accuracy

## ⚙️ Methodology

1. **Guideline Review**
   Established clear annotation rules for sentiment, topic classification, and confidence levels.

2. **Data Labeling**
   Labeled text samples across multiple categories, including straightforward and ambiguous cases.

3. **Edge Case Handling**
   Identified and analyzed challenging inputs where sentiment or intent was not immediately clear.

4. **Quality Review**
   Evaluated consistency across all labeled data and refined decisions based on guideline alignment.

---

## 📊 Labeling Framework

Each data point was labeled using the following structure:

* **Sentiment**: Positive | Neutral | Negative
* **Topic**: Product | Customer Service | Delivery | Other
* **Confidence**: High | Medium | Low

This structured approach ensures clarity, consistency, and usability for downstream AI systems.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* **Outcome outweighs tone** when determining sentiment
* **Weak language indicates neutral sentiment**, not uncertainty
* **Ambiguity lowers confidence**, not accuracy
* **Consistency across similar examples is critical** for high-quality datasets
